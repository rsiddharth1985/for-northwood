A solo trick-taking game

Score thresholds (all modes):
🥇 Gold — 20 stars  |  🥈 Silver — 18 stars
🥉 Bronze — 16 stars  |  💔 Loss — under 16

📖 How to Play

🎯 Goal

Peacefully unite Northwood's eight fiefs by visiting each one and convincing its ruler through dialogue. Win each ruler over by ending the visit with exactly the right score — then earn their victory stars. The more stars you finish with, the higher your medal.

🌱 Basic Game — Setup

Recommended for your first few games.

  • Place the 8 fief cards in a row, numbered 0 to 7 left to right. Their target scores match their number (Fief 0 = score 0, Fief 7 = score 7). Their VP stars range from 4 (edges) down to 1 (centre).
  • Take the 4 Jack cards (one per suit) and place them as your allies.
  • Shuffle the 8 King & Queen cards and deal one face-up onto each fief as its ruler. Offset it upward so the fief number is still visible.
  • Shuffle the 32 dialogue cards (values 1–8 in four suits: 🐾 Claws, 🌸 Flowers, 🍃 Leaves, 👁 Eyes) to form the draw deck.
  • Draw 8 cards from the deck into your hand.

🌲 Full Game (Randomized) — Setup

Uses all 24 character cards. Shuffle them all, then for each suit deal in order:

  • The first card of that suit revealed becomes your ally for that suit.
  • The next two cards of that suit become rulers (placed on fiefs).
  • Any further cards of that suit are set aside unused.

Once you have 4 allies and 8 rulers, shuffle the rulers and deal one to each fief. Then shuffle the 32 dialogue cards and draw 8 into your hand.

🎭 Full Game (Selected) — Setup

Same as Randomized, but you choose the characters. For each of the four suits:

  • Pick 1 character to be your ally — their ability is available every visit.
  • Pick 2 characters to be rulers — they will be randomly placed on the 8 fiefs (2 per suit).

The remaining characters are set aside. Then shuffle the 8 rulers, deal one to each fief, and draw 8 dialogue cards into your hand.

🗺 Each Visit — Flow

  1. Choose an unvisited fief. You can change your mind before the first dialogue begins.
  2. Optionally bring in friendly rulers as ally substitutes (rulers won in previous visits). Each substitute replaces one ally slot for this visit only and is removed afterward whether used or not.
  3. Play dialogues (tricks) — see below — until either your hand or the draw deck is empty.
  4. Count your score pile. If it equals the fief's target exactly, you win the ruler over (they become friendly and can substitute in future visits). Otherwise, the ruler is removed from the game and the fief is lost.
  5. Reset: reshuffle all 32 dialogue cards, draw 8 new cards, and choose the next fief.

⚔️ Dialogues (Tricks)

Before each dialogue you may exhaust one ally to use their ability (once per ally per visit; all reset between visits). Then:

  • The top card of the deck is flipped face-up as the ruler's statement and placed in the discard pile.
  • You play one card from your hand as your response. You must follow suit (play the same suit as the statement) if you can. If you have no cards of that suit, you may play anything.
  • You score a point (move your card to the score pile) if either:
    • Your card is the same suit as the statement and higher in value, OR
    • The statement is not the ruler's trump suit, but your card is the trump suit.
  • If you don't score, your response card goes to the discard pile.

Example: Ruler is 👁 Eyes (trump). Ruler plays 5🐾. You have no 🐾 cards, so you may play anything. Playing a 👁 card scores (trump). Playing 8🌸 does not score (neither statement suit nor trump).

🧙 All Ally Abilities

Exhaust an ally (click it) before a ruler's statement to use its ability once per visit.

Jacks

  • Jack of Claws: Draw cards until you have 8 in hand.
  • Jack of Flowers: Discard all cards of the ruler's trump suit from your hand.
  • Jack of Leaves: Swap the current ruler with any unvisited ruler up to 2 fiefs away. Only the ruler (and trump suit) moves — the target score stays.
  • Jack of Eyes: Draw 2 cards, then discard 2 cards.

Kings

  • King of Claws: Put the highest-valued card(s) from your hand into the score pile.
  • King of Flowers: Discard two cards from your hand whose values sum to exactly 8.
  • King of Leaves: Look at the top card of the deck. Optionally swap it with a card in your hand.
  • King of Eyes: Name a suit. Draw 2 cards, then discard all cards of that suit from your hand.

Queens

  • Queen of Claws: Move the top card of the score pile to the top of the deck, then move the top card of the discard pile to the score pile.
  • Queen of Flowers: Use the current ruler's ability as if they were your ally.
  • Queen of Leaves: Draw 2 cards, then discard 1 card.
  • Queen of Eyes: Look at the top 3 cards of the deck. Keep them in the same order or shuffle the deck.

Princes

  • Prince of Claws: Draw cards one at a time. Stop whenever you choose — but you must stop if you draw the trump suit.
  • Prince of Flowers: For each value 1–8, keep at most one card of that value in hand. Discard all duplicates (you choose which copy to keep).
  • Prince of Leaves: Move this visit to the next higher or lower unvisited fief (new ruler and target apply).
  • Prince of Eyes: Discard a card then draw a card. Repeat once for each other exhausted ally.

Barons

  • Baron of Claws: Choose a card in hand. Search the deck for all cards of that value, draw them, then reshuffle the deck.
  • Baron of Flowers: Discard all cards with value 1, 2, or 3 from your hand.
  • Baron of Leaves: Optionally swap a hand card with the top of the discard pile (same value), and/or the top of the score pile (same value).
  • Baron of Eyes: Your next response — if its value is even, draw 1 card after responding; if odd, discard 1 card instead.

Ladies

  • Lady of Claws: Discard your lowest-valued card (your choice if tied). Draw cards equal to its value.
  • Lady of Flowers: Discard one card whose value equals the number of cards currently in your hand.
  • Lady of Leaves: Draw 1 card, then unexhaust (refresh) one other exhausted ally.
  • Lady of Eyes: Draw 2 cards, then discard any cards from your hand that sum to exactly those two cards' combined value.

🏆 Scoring & Medals

After all 8 fiefs are visited, add up the VP stars of every friendly fief. Fiefs whose ruler was called in as a substitute still count.

  • 🥇 Gold — 20 stars (perfect game)
  • 🥈 Silver — 18–19 stars
  • 🥉 Bronze — 16–17 stars
  • 💔 Loss — 15 stars or fewer

Maximum possible: 4+4+3+3+2+2+1+1 = 20 stars. The edge fiefs (0 and 7) are hardest to win but worth the most.

💡 Tips

  • Your score must be exact — overshooting is just as bad as falling short. Plan ahead.
  • Ally abilities reset every visit — use them freely, don't hoard them.
  • Winning easier fiefs first gives you friendly rulers to substitute later, unlocking stronger abilities for hard fiefs.
  • The target-0 fief is won by scoring zero points — deliberately losing every dialogue.
